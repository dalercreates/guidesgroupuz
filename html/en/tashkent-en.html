Our route:

Monument of Courage

The Monument of Courage in Tashkent is an architectural structure erected in honor of the memory of the victims of the earthquake that occurred in Tashkent in 1966. This monument was opened in 1976 and is a symbol of the courage and unity of the Uzbek people in the fight against the tragic consequences of a natural disaster. The monument is a 26-meter column decorated with ceramic mosaics, on top of which there is a torch, symbolizing hope and light in dark times. Around the column are fountains and green spaces, creating an atmosphere of peace and tranquility.

Khast-Imam complex

The Khast-Imam complex in Tashkent is a historical and religious complex that includes several architectural monuments, including the Khast-Imam mosque, the Hazrat Imam mausoleum, the Barak-Khan madrasah and the library. The complex is one of the main centers of Islamic culture and heritage in Uzbekistan. The Khast-Imam mosque, built in the 16th century, is the largest mosque in the city and one of the main Islamic shrines in the country. The Mausoleum of Hazrat Imam, built in the 16th century, serves as a place of pilgrimage for Muslims from all over the world, as it houses the remains of the Islamic prophet and Caliph Ali and his brother Kusain. Khast-Imam complex is one of the main tourist attractions in Tashkent and attracts thousands of visitors every year.

Ancient market "Chorsu"

Chorsu Market is a historical market in the center of Tashkent that has existed for more than two millennia. It is one of the largest and most popular markets in the city and is a marketplace for locals and tourists. You can find anything in the market, from fresh fruits and vegetables to traditional Uzbek clothes and souvenirs. In addition, the market has many cafes and restaurants where you can taste the local cuisine. The Chorsu market is also a historical landmark of the city and is a real cultural treasury where you can get acquainted with Uzbek culture and traditions.

Monument to the "Victims of Repression"

The "Victims of Repression" Monument in Tashkent is a monument erected in honor of the thousands of victims of political repression who were executed and buried in mass graves on the territory of Uzbekistan from the 1920s to the 1950s. The monument was opened in 2002 and is a symbol of sorrow and respect for the dead. The monument is a memorial wall engraved with the names and dates of the victims of repression. In the center of the wall is a figure of a man who keeps his hands on his knees, symbolizing grief and memory of the dead. The "Victims of Repressions" monument is an important place for all those who want to honor the memory of the victims of political repressions and preserve the historical memory of the tragic events of the past.

New part of the city "Independence Square"

The new part of the city "Independence Square" in Tashkent is a modern area located in the center of the capital of Uzbekistan. The area was built in the early 2000s as part of a program to modernize and develop the city. There are tall buildings, numerous fountains and sculptures, as well as parks and squares on the square. In the center of the square is a monument to Islam Karimov, the first president of Uzbekistan, who initiated the creation of a new district. Independence Square is an important symbol of the development and modernity of Tashkent and attracts many tourists and guests of the capital.

Palace of the Romanovs

The Romanov Palace in Tashkent is a historical monument built at the beginning of the 20th century by order of the Governor-General of the Turkestan Territory Nikolai Romanov. It served as a residence for the head of state and high-ranking officials during their visits to Tashkent. The palace combines elements of Russian and Central Asian architecture, decorated with luxurious interiors and gardens. Today it is one of the main tourist sites of the city, attracting lovers of history and culture.

Theater "Alisher Navoi"

The Alisher Navoi Theater in Tashkent is one of the most famous theaters in Uzbekistan. It was founded in 1947 and named after the famous Uzbek poet and playwright Alisher Navoi. The theater is the center of cultural life in Tashkent and hosts many high-quality productions in the Uzbek language, including dramas, comedies and musicals. The theater is also an important venue for festivals and other events related to the arts and culture of Uzbekistan.

Square "Amir Temur"

Amir Temur Square in Tashkent is one of the most famous and beautiful parks in the city. Named after Amir Temur, a famous Turkmen warrior and commander who ruled in the 14th century. There is a lot of greenery, flowers, fountains in the square, as well as a monument to Amir Temur himself. The square is a popular place for recreation and walking, both for locals and tourists. It also hosts various events such as concerts, exhibitions and festivals.
